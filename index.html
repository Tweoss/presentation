Future Letter

You won't remember this, so to give you the context, this letter is from the final AP Literature project. You had to write a letter to yourself for the first day of school. Now, your past self would simply like to remind you of your past self's ideals.

First, don't make a scene. Try to be unobtrusive, at least for the first week or so. Make friends but know that the situation is in no way certain yet. Second, try things! Don't be a recluse, there's nothing to gain there. Joining in on the inevitable networking is going to be way more exciting than not. Third, keep some time for yourself. Don't look at homework or your phone or your computer, and just sit and think for a bit everyday.

Favorite Book

Flowers for Algernon

It's been several years, but I still remember how striking the book was to me. The concept of breathtaking ascent is implied everywhere in our lives (yes, that includes the American Dream), and the inevitable loss that the main character goes through is incredibly painful. The novel really is about a compressed life and so it has a dense emotional impact.
